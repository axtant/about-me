Setting the Scene

I was sitting idle on a Discord server when a friend dropped a reel in chat — a cute ad about how to make your Valentine feel special. You know the type.

Something clicked. I wanted to build my own self-hosted version of that. The timing? February 1st. Exactly 14 days before Valentine's Day. Challenge accepted.

The Task Was Simple

  • User enters their question
  • Adds multiple answer options
  • Selects one as the "correct" answer
  • Shares the generated link — a choice they won't be able to refuse 😉

Technical Details

  • Domain — Namecheap
  • Framework — Vue.js
  • Database — None. No data, no database, no problem.
  • Hosting — GCP Firebase
  • AI-powered suggestions — because why not? Fallback questions included in case the API fails.

The full repo is open source at github.com/axtant/bee-my-valentine.

Now Let's Try Selling It

Web-friendly. Phone-friendly. So — why not Shopify?

The curiosity pushed me to spin up a Meta Ads account and run 5 campaigns across different age groups, platforms, and ad formats. Real question: would people actually buy this?

  • Day 0–1 (testing days) — ₹2,899 spent · 4 orders
  • Day 2–4 (gaining attention) — ₹3,000 spent · 11 orders
  • Day 5–6 (gaining even more) — ₹2,500 spent · 17 orders

Conclusion

This was my first-ever experience selling a digital product through paid ads and reaching a mass audience. And honestly? It was a rush.

Generated around ₹5,000 in revenue initially, then got a boost on Valentine's Day itself — bumping profits to ₹6,400. The ad costs to reach the right audience were higher though, which ultimately resulted in a small loss.

My goal of earning lakhs through digital products is still very much alive. I'm still on the path of learning and understanding the market better.

In the end, fulfilling 32 orders and helping 64+ people connect more closely made this Valentine's truly special for me.
